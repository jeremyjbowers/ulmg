Statcast park factors show the apparent effect of each displayed statistic based on offensive events in the selected park. These statistics are normalized such that 100 is the average. The park-specific number is generated by looking at each batter and pitcher, controlled by handedness, and then comparing the frequency of that metric in the selected park compared to those exact players's performance in other parks. For example: A HR mark of 135 for Great American Ball Park does not mean that the Reds hit 35% more home runs at their home park. It means that for all batters and pitchers who played at Great American Ball Park, 35% more home run events were observed at Great American Ball Park than at the average park.
